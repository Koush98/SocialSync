Last updated: April 6, 2026

1. Information we collect

We may collect account identifiers, connected social account names, OAuth tokens, media metadata, scheduled post content, and operational logs required to run the publishing workflow.

2. How we use information

  • Connect user-authorized social media accounts
  • Store and schedule publishing tasks
  • Upload and attach media to planned content
  • Track publishing status, errors, and operational activity
  • Support tenant-level separation and security

3. Platform access

When a user connects a social account, the application requests only the permissions needed for approved publishing and account management features.

4. Token storage

Access and refresh tokens are stored in encrypted form where supported by the application and used only for connected account workflows.

5. Tenant isolation

Workspace data is tenant-scoped so one tenant cannot access another tenant’s connected accounts, media, or post records.

6. Data retention

We retain connected account records, media references, and publishing history for operational use, troubleshooting, and audit purposes, subject to retention policy and account deletion requests.

7. Data sharing

We do not sell personal information. Data is shared only with connected social platforms and infrastructure/services required to operate the product.

8. Contact

For privacy-related questions, contact the SnapKey team or the administrator responsible for the connected workspace environment.
